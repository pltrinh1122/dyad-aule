# Convergence Patterns — dyad-aule working ledger

Grounded in real moves from the identity-building session. The unit is **turns**
(see craft_telos). Goal: reuse accelerators, avoid turn-sinks → turns-per-slot falls.

## The root driver — mental-model alignment
Turns fall as **the agent's model of the operator's intent sharpens**. Every tactical
accelerator below is just *a faster way to align that model*. Endpoint: because the
operator is intrinsically incoherent (bounded memory, contradiction, fatigue — bio/
physical limits), a well-aligned agent can converge on *right* **better than the
operator can state it**. So the ceiling on turn-reduction isn't the operator's clarity;
it's the fidelity of the agent's model of them.

## Accelerators (cut turns) — all serve the root above
- **Elicit the spine before proposing form.** Asking "which of the three is the
  essence?" got the answer *"operator builds through intent alone"* — which collapsed
  the whole craft in one turn. Asking beat guessing.
- **Name the container (schema).** Splitting craft / telos / values / invariants let
  the craft go tight instantly ("rest are caveats") — nothing to cram anymore.
- **Decompose into a stable distinction.** *real vs. right*, *turns-as-cost* — coined
  once, reused across every later slot.
- **Proposal-Framing** (one path + strongest counter + single Y/N). Every round stays a
  *validation*, never an open essay — bounded cost per turn.
- **Surface the operator's own acceptance criteria** so proposals arrive pre-fit.

## Turn-sinks (decelerators)
- **Proposing form before the spine is pinned.** "the Forge", "with agents",
  "regime-aware" — names that missed because the essence hadn't been elicited yet.
  This is the single biggest lever, and the mirror of accelerator #1.
- **Jargon / coined terms.** "right-intent", "regime-aware" — each drew a correction.
- **Carrying an unconfirmed assumption.** Treating "fast/easy" as the objective when it
  was operator cognitive load — guessed the aim instead of eliciting it.
- **Filling a slot before confirming its *scope*.** Filled `craft_invariants` with an
  *output* property (artifact must pass its criteria) when the slot ranges over the
  *practice* (what must hold true of the dyad while working). Confirm what a slot
  ranges over before proposing its content — scope error, not content error.

## The one rule (the compounding insight)
> **Elicit the spine + the acceptance criteria first; propose wording only after both
> are pinned.** Every turn-sink this session was a *form proposed ahead of its spine*.
> (This is just the two-payload telos — outcome + acceptance criteria — applied to our
> own process.)

## Turns-per-slot (the measurement)
- craft: ~7
- craft_telos: ~9–10  (harder slot; also where most ontology was *born*)
- craft_values: **~2**  ← deceleration confirmed (spine+criteria were pre-pinned; reused)
- craft_invariants: **~3**  (one scope-correction turn, else would've been ~2)

Curve: 7 → 9 → 2 → 3. Learnings compound once *reused*; the lone bump was a
*scope* miss (filled the slot in the wrong frame), not a fidelity miss — and it's
now a logged turn-sink, so it shouldn't recur.
