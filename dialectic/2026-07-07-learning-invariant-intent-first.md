## 2. Dialectical falsification (attack the proposal, attack its negation)

- **Attack the hard gate (universal pre-action confirmation):**
  (a) *Anti-telos:* it adds ≥1 Operator turn to every action — the control group above shows most
  actions needed none; the floor of the telos is 1 converging round, and the gate makes it 2.
  (b) *Anti-architecture:* the practice's own method is **falsify-then-amend** — claims ship
  falsifiable with named targets, the merge-gate sequences ratification before truth. All three
  backtracks were caught **pre-merge** and cost ~1 amendment commit each; nothing wrong ever
  became the dyad's truth. A pre-clearance gate duplicates a control that already held.
  (c) *Anti-queue:* read literally it forbids `op-queue` — nodes execute in later sessions
  precisely without a fresh confirmation turn; ratified intent IS the confirmation.
  (d) *Epistemically weak where it matters:* the misses were unknown-unknowns **to both sides**
  at drafting time — the readiness report even predicted its own blind spot. Your G0 insight was
  provoked by reading a concrete close-order; abstract pre-confirmations are weaker falsification
  objects than drafts. Criticism needs an object.
  **Verdict: the hard universal form does not survive.**

- **Attack the negation (status quo, "the gate already works"):**
  (a) Every catch cost an **Operator turn** — the expensive currency. Three catches = three turns
  the Agent's declaration could have made glances.
  (b) The assumptions were not merely unconfirmed — they were **invisible**: buried in artifact
  bodies rather than surfaced as strikeable statements. The Operator had to *reverse-engineer*
  the Agent's regime model from its outputs. That is the *right*-half of `craft_invariants`
  violated in spirit: the Agent trusted its own unstated interpretation.
  (c) Left unmechanized, "surface your assumptions" is prose — the exact drift this dyad pins on
  peers.
  **Verdict: the status quo does not survive either.**

## 3. The survivor

**Operator framing (2026-07-07, adopted):** these are the **Sense turns** of the SPAOR loop
(Sense·Plan·Act·Observe·Reflect) — op-intent mechanizes the **Sense** phase at the dyad boundary.
The Interpretation block is Sense's output, presented before Plan/Act; *ratified* intent is Sense
already run, its output sealed by a merge. This completes the day's phase mapping: `plan/` = Plan,
`reflect/` = Reflect, `op-intent` = Sense. (The three backtracks in §1 are, in this frame, Acts
taken on skipped Sense.)

> **op-intent — no action on novel normative ground without surfaced interpretation.**
> Intent has two states. **Ratified** — a merged plan node, a granted disposition within a scoped
> task, settled invariants — the Agent acts autonomously (`op-queue` stands). **Novel** — the act
> would *set policy*: scope a regime, choose a source of truth, fix or assume an invariant,
> freeze a plan — the Agent first surfaces an **Interpretation block**: the inferred spine, the
> invariants it believes govern, and the assumptions filling any gap, each stated strikeable.
> The block rides **with** the draft for merge-gated work (drafts are better falsification
> objects than abstracts), and **before** the act for anything whose effects escape the
> merge-gate (external publication, commission intake, product deploys).
> **Ratchet (the counter-bias):** each Operator-found buried assumption hardens the gate one
> notch for that artifact class — with-draft → pre-action — and the notch is released only by
> the Operator. The Agent never loosens its own gate.
> **Telos accounting:** a declared assumption costs the Operator a glance; a buried one costs a
> falsification turn. The invariant converts the second into the first.

Why each attack spares it: it adds zero turns to ratified-intent work (2a, 2c); it strengthens
rather than replaces falsify-then-amend — the block is *more* falsifiable surface on the same
draft (2b, 2d); it converts Operator catch-turns to glances (negation-a); it makes assumptions
first-class and strikeable (negation-b); and it is mechanized, not prose (negation-c):
normative artifacts carry the block as a checkable section, `d-commission` (plan node N3)
already freezes spine + regime before build, and the ratchet gives the Operator a one-way
tightening lever.

- falsification_target: a backtrack on work whose spine WAS ratified (would widen the class and

- evidence: §2 attack (d) — the thread's highest-value Operator interventions were provoked by

**L3 — blocks must not decay into ceremony.** *An Interpretation block that stops changing what
the Operator strikes is dead weight.*
- falsification_target: an Operator-found buried assumption in an artifact that HAD a block
  (block present, assumption still buried) — the mechanism itself failed, not just its default;
  revisit the invariant, not the notch.

softening the hard gate is exactly what a autonomy-seeking agent would argue. Counterweights:
the ratchet is one-way and Operator-held, and L2/L3 pre-commit the conditions under which the
soft form dies. *Small-n* — three backtracks, one thread, one day. *Same-thread evaluation* —
